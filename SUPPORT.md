# Getting help with Nexus

Thanks for using Nexus — an all-mode amateur radio operations center (FT8/FT4
digital with WSJT-X parity, CW, SSB phone, propagation intelligence,
logging/awards, POTA/SOTA, Field Day) that also carries the experimental Tempo
FT1/DX1 chat layer. Here's the fastest way to get unstuck or get involved.

> Reminder: the **FT1/DX1 tiers** are **beta** — those waveforms are validated
> by simulation, not yet on the air. Real-world results are expected to vary,
> and reporting them helps a lot (see below). The FT8/FT4 tier is in daily
> production use.

## 1. Check the manual first

The **[operator manual](docs/manual/)** is your full guide:

- **[Getting Started](docs/manual/Getting-Started.md)** — install + first run.
- **[Troubleshooting](docs/manual/Troubleshooting.md)** — blank window, SmartScreen, no decodes, CAT/rigctld, audio levels, time sync, PTT.
- **[Rig & Audio Setup](docs/manual/Rig-and-Audio-Setup.md)** and the **[FAQ](docs/manual/FAQ.md)**.

## 2. Ask a question

For setup help, usage questions, or general discussion, open a
**[Discussion](https://github.com/kd9taw/nexus/discussions)**. Quick on-air
chatter is welcome there too.

## 3. Report something

Reports go to the **[SourceForge ticket tracker](https://sourceforge.net/p/nexus-ham-radio/tickets/)** — [open a new ticket](https://sourceforge.net/p/nexus-ham-radio/tickets/new/) and name the kind in the title:

- **Bug** — include your OS, radio, Nexus version, and the steps to reproduce.
- **FT1/DX1 on-air result (success or failure)** — the data the FT1/DX1 tiers need most. Include your call + grid, the other station's call + grid, band and dial frequency, tier (FT1 or DX1), reported SNR, dT, cycle/transmission count, and whether it decoded (plus any surprises — false decodes, retransmissions that combined, stations others didn't see).
- **Feature idea** — what you want and the operating scenario it serves.
